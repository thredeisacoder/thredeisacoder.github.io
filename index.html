About Me _

I am Threde Nolan, a passionate 4th year Computer Science student with a specialization in cybersecurity and web penetration testing. My journey into the world of ethical hacking began during my freshman year when I discovered the fascinating balance between breaking and securing digital systems.

Currently pursuing my degree, I have developed strong skills in identifying and exploiting web vulnerabilities, implementing security measures, and conducting thorough security assessments. I've participated in several CTF competitions, including HackTheBox and TryHackMe challenges, consistently ranking in the top percentiles.

Outside of academics, I actively contribute to my university's cybersecurity club where I lead workshops on web application security and mentor junior students. I'm also an active participant in bug bounty programs, having responsibly disclosed vulnerabilities to several organizations.

My goal is to bridge the gap between theoretical security concepts and practical implementation, helping organizations build more resilient web applications. I am constantly learning new techniques and tools to stay ahead in the ever-evolving field of web security.
